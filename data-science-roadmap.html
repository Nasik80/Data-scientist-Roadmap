01
Phase One
Mathematics & Statistics Foundation
MONTHS 1–2
3–4 hrs/day

Data Science is applied mathematics. Without a solid foundation here, everything else feels like magic — not skill.

📐 Linear Algebra

  • Vectors and vector spaces
  • Matrices and matrix operations
  • Eigenvalues & Eigenvectors
  • Dot product, cross product
  • Matrix decomposition (SVD, PCA)

📊 Statistics — Core

  • Mean, Median, Mode, Variance, SD
  • Probability basics & Bayes theorem
  • Distributions: Normal, Binomial, Poisson
  • Hypothesis testing (t-test, chi-square)
  • p-values, confidence intervals
  • Correlation vs Causation

📈 Calculus (for ML)

  • Derivatives & partial derivatives
  • Chain rule (backpropagation basis)
  • Gradient & gradient descent
  • Integrals (basic understanding)

🎲 Probability Theory

  • Events, sample space, axioms
  • Conditional probability
  • Joint & marginal distributions
  • Expected value & variance
  • Law of Large Numbers, CLT

📊 Data Visualization

🧹 Exploratory Data Analysis

  • Outlier detection
  • Distribution analysis
  • Correlation heatmaps

Daily Routine
How to Spend
4–6 Hours a Day

Consistency beats intensity. A disciplined daily schedule across 12–18 months is what transforms a beginner into a job-ready Data Scientist.

BLOCK 1 · 60–90 MIN
Concept Learning
Watch one focused tutorial, read one documentation page, or study one textbook chapter. No multitasking. Full attention only.
BLOCK 2 · 60–90 MIN
Hands-on Coding
Open a Jupyter Notebook and implement what was learned. No copy-pasting from tutorials — type every line. Break things and fix them.
BLOCK 3 · 45–60 MIN
SQL + Python Practice
Solve 2–3 SQL problems on LeetCode or HackerRank. Or work through a Pandas exercise set. Builds interview muscle memory.
BLOCK 4 · 60 MIN
Project Work
Dedicate time to an ongoing Kaggle competition or personal project. Even 1 commit per day = an impressive GitHub streak over months.
BLOCK 5 · 30 MIN
Review & Notes
Write what was learned today in own words (Notion, Obsidian, or physical notebook). Spaced repetition = long-term retention.
WEEKENDS
Deep Dives
Use weekends for Kaggle competitions, watching full 2-hr conference talks, reading research papers, or building a mini project from scratch.

  1. Install: langchain, faiss-cpu, pypdf, openai or use free Groq API
  2. Load PDF using PyPDFLoader from LangChain
  3. Split text into chunks: RecursiveCharacterTextSplitter (chunk_size=500)
  4. Embed chunks using HuggingFace sentence-transformers (free)
  5. Store embeddings in FAISS vector database
  6. On user query: embed question → find top-5 similar chunks
  7. Pass chunks + question to LLM (Groq llama3 = free + fast)
  8. Display answer + source paragraph in Streamlit
  9. Add chat history using st.session_state

Inside Knowledge
Critical Tips Specific
to the Indian Market
TIP — 01
Start as a Data Analyst
Most people fail chasing "Data Scientist" titles immediately. Start as a Data Analyst (easier to get), gain 1–2 years of real business experience, then transition. The pay jump is worth it.
TIP — 02
Kaggle Rank Is a Resume
Indian recruiters, especially at product companies, respect Kaggle rankings. A Kaggle Expert or Master badge can unlock interviews at top companies without a traditional degree.
TIP — 03
Target Product Companies
Service companies (TCS, Infosys, Wipro) hire data scientists but offer lower salaries and less growth. Target Flipkart, Swiggy, Razorpay, PhonePe, CRED, Meesho, Zepto, and MNC R&D centers in India.
TIP — 04
Domain Knowledge Multiplies Value
A data scientist who understands finance, healthcare, or e-commerce deeply earns significantly more. Pick a domain, read industry reports, and become the person who bridges business and models.
TIP — 05
Build in Public
Post on LinkedIn about projects built, papers read, and problems solved. 3–4 posts per month showing genuine work creates inbound interest from recruiters. It compounds over time.
TIP — 06
Never Stop at Accuracy Metrics
Interviewers in India test business thinking. Always explain models in terms of business impact (revenue, churn reduction, fraud prevention). Pure technical answers impress fewer hiring managers than expected.
TIP — 07
The Free Path Is Enough
80% of the required learning can be done for free: Coursera (audit mode), YouTube (3Blue1Brown, StatQuest, Sentdex, Krish Naik), Kaggle, fast.ai, and GitHub. Paid bootcamps are not necessary if self-disciplined.
TIP — 08
Talk to Working Data Scientists
Join communities: Kaggle forums, DataHack by Analytics Vidhya, DAIR.AI Discord, LinkedIn groups, and local PyData meetups. Conversations with practitioners accelerate learning faster than any course.
TIP — 09
Projects Must Solve Real Problems
Avoid generic Titanic/Iris projects. Build projects around Indian context: IPL match prediction, real estate price prediction for a specific city, crop yield forecasting, or credit scoring for Indian banks.
